Phonemic echoes in Finnegans Wake

Rhymes, assonance, alliteration and consonant echoes counted on IPA phonemes within sentences — the poetic machinery of Joyce's prose, in numbers.

1. Method

All echoes are counted on IPA phonemes (transcription as on the "music" subpage), within sentences, with function words excluded from the rhyme and consonant-echo rankings. Exact rhyme = identical phonemes from the last stressed vowel to the end of the word — so words rhyme by pronunciation, not by spelling. 15,061 sentences analysed.

3. Assonance — vowel harmony

I count assonance as a run: a series of consecutive words in one sentence sharing the same peak (stressed) vowel. The number at each item is the length of that single run — not the phoneme's corpus frequency (tens of thousands of words carry these vowels; /ɪ/ is the most frequent phoneme of the text). Runs of ≥3 words found: 1,386; the longest below:
