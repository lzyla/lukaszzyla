The music of Finnegans Wake in IPA

The sound layer of the novel described with the categories of the International Phonetic Alphabet: phonemes instead of letters, articulation instead of orthography, sonority and lexical stress as measures of "musicality".

1. Method and coverage

I transcribed the text into IPA phonemes: 80.54% of word occurrences were found in the CMU pronouncing dictionary (ARPABET mapped onto IPA); the remaining 19.46% — Joyce's coinages — I transcribed with grapheme-to-phoneme rules (an approximation of English pronunciation; a heuristic, not a philological transcription). Total: 869,056 phonemes.

2. Phoneme inventory

Vowels make up 38.8% of all phonemes. Most frequent phonemes: /ɪ/ 6.779%, /n/ 6.614%, /t/ 5.961%, /s/ 5.843%, /ɹ/ 5.603%, /l/ 4.825%, /ə/ 4.775%, /ɛ/ 4.703%, /d/ 4.386%, /æ/ 4.209%, /ʌ/ 3.217%, /m/ 3.15%, /k/ 3.02%, /ɑ/ 2.962%.

5. Sonority — the "melody" of words

The sonority hierarchy (the higher, the more "singable"; scale 1–10): open vowels 10 … close 8, approximants /j w ɹ/ 7, lateral /l/ 6, nasals /m n ŋ/ 5, voiced fricatives 4 / voiceless 3, affricates 2.5, voiced plosives 2 / voiceless 1. Examples: /ɑ/ 10, /ɛ/ 9, /i/ 8, /j/ 7, /l/ 6, /n/ 5, /z/ 4, /s/ 3, /tʃ/ 2.5, /b/ 2, /p/ 1.

Mean sonority (per distinct word) of the book's English words: 5.531; of Joyce's coinages: 5.736 — the invented words are more sonorous than the ordinary English of the same book.

The most "singing" coinages (mean sonority): waaaaaa (9.571), aiaiaiai (9), aweigh (9), areyou (8.8), awallow (8.75), youyou (8.75), uwayoei (8.667), woewoewoe (8.625), alleyou (8.6), wararrow (8.5), allower (8.4), narrowa (8.4), amorrow (8.4), ouryour (8.4).
